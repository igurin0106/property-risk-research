# Japan Municipal Zoning Data - Official Sources Reference

## National Resources

### MLIT National Spatial Data Portal
- **URL:** http://nlftp.mlit.go.jp/
- **Japanese:** 国土交通省 国土数値情報
- **Coverage:** Zoning maps and urban planning data for all designated cities
- **Format:** GIS shapefiles, free download
- **Limitation:** Requires GIS software to view; not all municipalities update frequently

### Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism
- **Agency:** 国土交通省
- **Zoning Database:** https://www.mlit.go.jp/
- **Building Standards:** 建築基準法 (Building Standards Act)
- **Resources:** Searchable building code requirements by municipality

## Research Strategy for Missing Data

### Priority 1: Direct Official Contact
- Send formal information request (情報公開請求) to city planning division
- Request: "該当地区の建ぺい率と容積率のデータ" (zoning data for specified area)
- Response time: Typically 2-4 weeks

### Priority 2: Local Real Estate Associations
- Contact: 不動産協会 (Real Estate Association)
- Many local associations have zoning databases
- May provide verified, current data

### Priority 3: Site Visits & Field Research
- For Nagasaki, Takamatsu, and other smaller cities
- Interview local real estate agents and city planners
- Photograph zoning signs (common on building sites)

### Priority 4: Academic Papers
- Search: Japan Society of Civil Engineers (土木学会)
- University urban planning research centers often have databases
- Google Scholar + Japanese city name + "用途地域" or "容積率"

## Data Verification Checklist

Before using zoning data for property decisions:
- [ ] Source document date (within last 3 years?)
- [ ] Fire zone data current? (Check latest city ordinance)
- [ ] Address-specific verification needed for:
  - [ ] Historic preservation zones
  - [ ] Earthquake-resistant zones
  - [ ] Flood hazard zones
  - [ ] Development restricted areas
- [ ] Local government confirmation obtained?
- [ ] Site visit or photo verification?

## Important Limitations

1. **Data Freshness:** City zoning ordinances updated 1-3 times per year
2. **GIS Access:** Some cities restrict detailed map access without registration
3. **Translation:** No official English-language zoning ordinances available
4. **Micro-variations:** Building-by-building zoning differences not captured
5. **Overlay Zones:** Special restrictions (preservation, earthquake, flood) not included
6. **Historical Data:** Past zoning versions needed for property development timelines
