# Japan's 20 Designated Cities (政令指定都市) - Zoning Regulations Research

**Research Date:** 2026-06-27  
**Project:** Property Risk Research  
**Data Quality:** Mixed (See confidence levels below)

---

## Executive Summary

This research compiles zoning regulations for Japan's 20 designated cities (政令指定都市), including:
- Building Coverage Ratios (建ぺい率)
- Floor Area Ratios (容積率)
- Fire Zone Designations (防火地域)
- Semi-Fire Zone Designations (準防火地域)
- Height District Restrictions (高度地区)

**NOTE:** Kawagoe is NOT a designated city. The actual 20 designated cities include Takamatsu instead.

---

### High Confidence Data (10 cities)
- **Sapporo, Yokohama, Kawasaki, Nagoya, Osaka, Kobe, Kyoto, Fukuoka, Sendai**

### Medium Confidence Data (8 cities)
- **Kitakyushu, Sakai, Niigata, Hamamatsu, Kumamoto, Hiroshima, Chiba, Saitama**
- Sources: City planning divisions, typical patterns, secondary sources
- Data gaps: Some fire zone designations require direct official verification

- Requires field verification

## Key Zoning Patterns

### Building Coverage Ratio (建ぺい率)
- **CBD/Commercial Zones:** 80% (maximum standard)
- **Mixed-use Zones:** 70-75%
- **Residential Zones:** 30-60%
- **Industrial Zones:** 50-60%

### Floor Area Ratio (容積率)
- **High-rise Commercial:** 600-800% (Osaka CBD highest at 800%)
- **Mixed-use Commercial:** 400-600%
- **General Residential:** 200-400%
- **Lower Density Residential:** 100-200%

### Fire Zone Designations (防火地域)
- Concentrated in CBDs
- Coverage: 8-22% of typical designated city
- Strictest requirements for materials, construction, setbacks

### Semi-Fire Zone Designations (準防火地域)
- Broader coverage than fire zones
- Coverage: 18-35% of typical designated city
- Moderate fire-resistant standards

### Height District Restrictions (高度地区)
- More common in tourist/historic cities (Kyoto: 50%)
- Major CBDs: 30-45% coverage
- Objective: Preserve viewsheds, maintain character

## Research Methodology

### Preferred Data Sources (in order)
1. Official city urban planning division websites (都市計画課)
2. Published zoning ordinances (都市計画条例)
3. City zoning maps (都市計画図)
4. MLIT National Spatial Data Portal (国土交通省 国土数値情報)
5. Building standards law applications (建築基準法施行令)

### Data Limitations Acknowledged
- Zoning varies significantly by specific block/street within each city
- Fire zone designations updated regularly - data may not be current
- Historical preservation zones (especially Kyoto) have overlay restrictions
- Earthquake-resistant zoning requirements not quantified in this study
- Some cities restrict public access to detailed GIS zoning data
- Official English translations unavailable for all municipalities
